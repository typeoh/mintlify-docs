Rasa is a conversational AI framework that helps you build, test, deploy, and continuously improve AI assistants — from simple FAQ bots to complex, multi-turn task-completion agents. Whether you prefer writing code, designing flows visually, or a mix of both, Rasa gives your team the tools to move fast without sacrificing control or reliability.

## What makes Rasa different

At the core of Rasa is **CALM** (Conversational AI with Language Models) — Rasa's dialogue engine. CALM uses language models to understand what users say, then executes your business logic deterministically. That means your assistant benefits from the fluency of modern LLMs while still following your rules faithfully, no matter how complex or high-stakes the task.

  <Card title="Rasa Studio (No-Code)" icon="paintbrush">

Both interfaces share the same underlying platform, so your team can collaborate seamlessly. A developer might write a custom Python action in Rasa Pro while a conversation designer polishes the dialogue flows in Rasa Studio — all within the same project.

## Who Rasa is for

Rasa is built for teams that need more than a generic chatbot. Specifically, you'll find it most valuable if you are:

- A **developer or ML engineer** who wants fine-grained control over the dialogue pipeline, custom integrations, and deployment infrastructure.
- A **conversation designer** who wants to build and iterate on conversational flows quickly using a visual editor.
- A **business user or product manager** who wants to review real conversations, track performance, and make improvements without waiting for engineering.

<Note>
  You don't need to choose one role. Many Rasa projects involve a small cross-functional team using both Rasa Pro and Rasa Studio in tandem.
</Note>

## The CALM dialogue engine

CALM is what separates Rasa from prompt-chaining approaches. Instead of relying solely on an LLM to decide what to do next, CALM gives you:

- **Structured flows** that define the steps your assistant takes to complete a task
- **Automatic handling** of real-world conversation patterns like topic changes, clarifications, corrections, and interjections
- **Deterministic execution** of your business logic, even when user inputs are unpredictable
- **Transparency** into every decision the assistant makes, making debugging and auditing straightforward
